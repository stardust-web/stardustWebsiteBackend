SUBSYSTEMS

S.T.A.R.D.U.ST comprises of 6 main subsystems

Attitude Determination and Control System



ADCS
This subsystem is responsible for stabilising, maintaining and controlling the attitude, or in simpler terms, the orientation of the satellite.
Based on this function, the satellite orients it's solar panels, antennas and particularly its payload instruments using actuators.

Electrical Power System



EPS
To excerpt and condition the energy source, for distributing required power to various sub-systems including detecting and mitigating faults in the following process

Ground Control And Communication System



G&C
This subsystem is responsible for ensuring telecommunication between the satellite & another system, which may be either another satellite or a ground station. It provides the interface between the satellite & ground systems. The basic communications subsystem consists of a transmitter, a receiver, an antenna & a RF diplexer.

OnBoard Command and Data Handling System.



ODHS
The Onboard data handling subsytem is the brain of the satellite and is responsible for tasks such as data operations and issuing commands to multiple subsytems on-board the satellite.

PAYLOAD



PAYLOAD
It refers to the actual function of the satellite. In other words, its the purpose of the satellite. Most satellite teams have a payload subsytem that works on deciding the purpose and the methodology of achieving this aim.

Structures and Thermal System



S&T
The structure subsystem maintains an overall mechanical integrity of the satellite. It ensures that all the components are supported and enables the satellite to withstand and launch loads. The thermal subsystem maintains thermal stability on board and ensures optimum performance of the satellite by monitoring the operational temperature of all components.
